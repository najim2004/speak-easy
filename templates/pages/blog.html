JLPT N5 Express: 90-Day Masterclass for Beginners

The introductory level of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT), designed for absolute beginners to establish a foundational understanding of the language

Course Details

There are basically 4 main topics that will be taught in this JLPT N5 course. Below is a brief list of them:

1. Writing Systems (The Basics)

  • Hiragana: Complete mastery of all 46 characters (used for native Japanese words and grammar).
  • Katakana: Complete mastery of all 46 characters (used for foreign names and loanwords like "Coffee").
  • Kanji: Approximately 100–120 basic characters (e.g., numbers, days, people, and common verbs like "eat" or "go").

2. Vocabulary & Expressions

  • Word Count: Around 800–1,000 common words.
  • Topics: Self-introduction, family, food, daily routines, shopping, weather, and time.

3. Grammar Foundations

  • Particles: Correct use of markers like は (wa), が (ga), を (o), に (ni), で (de).
  • Verb Tenses: Present and past forms (Polite ~masu form).
  • Adjectives: Using i-adjectives and na-adjectives correctly.
  • Sentence Structure: Understanding the Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) order.

4. Reading & Listening

  • Reading: Ability to read short, simple sentences and signs written in Hiragana, Katakana, and basic Kanji.
  • Listening: Comprehending slow, clear conversations about everyday topics (like classroom instructions or asking for directions).

Core Course

Module 1: Introduction to the Japanese Language – Basics of pronunciation, intonation, and common greetings.

Module 2: Writing Systems (Hiragana & Katakana) – Mastery of the two phonetic scripts, including stroke order and reading practice.

Module 3: Basic Kanji – Recognition and writing of approximately 100–120 essential characters (e.g., numbers, time, and common nouns).

Module 4: Grammar Foundations – Fundamental sentence structures (SOV), essential particles (like wa, ga, wo, ni), and basic verb/adjective conjugations.

Module 5: Vocabulary Development – Building a repertoire of 800–1,000 common words related to family, food, shopping, and daily routines.

Module 6: Listening & Speaking – Understanding slow, clear speech in everyday situations and practicing basic conversational drills.

Module 7: Reading Comprehension – Practice reading short, simple passages, signs, and notices using basic vocabulary and Kanji.

Module 8: JLPT N5 Exam Preparation – Mock tests, section-wise strategies, and time management tips for the official exam.
